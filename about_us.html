About Us

The Furniture Trades Provident and Benevolent Association (later renamed the Furniture Trades Benevolent Association) was formed in 1903. Shortly after its formation, it established a Cottage Village and Orphanage at Cobden Hill, Radlett, Hertfordshire to 'maintain and educate the orphan and necessitous children' of its members. The establishment was officially opened on 22nd July, 1905, by the Association's President, Samuel James Waring, who later to become the 1st Baron Waring, and it comprised of two semi-detached houses. By 1918, a third house had been built but in 1921 the Association relocated to premises in Highgate, London.









Harold Hunter Ward, who was born in the latter part of the nineteenth century and who had moved to Radlett, was an active Freemason being a member of Watford Lodge No.404 and Watford Chapter No.404. He was a joining member of Elstree Lodge No.3092 becoming Master in 1911 and 1926, he was appoitned to Provincial Grand Treasurer for 1916-17, he was a founding member of the Hertfordshier Master's Lodge No.4090 in 1920 and of Elstree Chapter No.3092 in 1925, and he was appointed to Past Grand Standard Bearer in 1934. When the Association moved out of Radlett in the early 1920's, Ward purchased the three properties. A private road, named The Rosewalk, was established between the two houses nearer to Cobden Hill, and these were converted into two detached houses which became No.1 and No.2 The Rosewalk. The third house, situated behind No.1, became No.3 The Rosewalk and eventually home to a number of Lodges, including Elstree Lodge. Thus, thanks to Ward's foresight, a Masonic meeting place was established in Radlett; the first recorded activity being the consecration of Elstree Chapter on the 25th July, 1925.











Ward died in the mid 1940's, afterwhich No.3 and the houses were purchsed by Donald Forrester, another enthusiastic and notable Freemason and philanthropist. Forrester was a member of Barham Lodge No.6004, becoming Master in 19??, and a joining member of Elstree Lodge No.3092. In 1959, the resident Units (Elstree, Radlett, Barham, and Caishowe Lodges and Elstree Chapter) formed the Masonic Hall (Radlett) Limited company and acquired the freehold to No.3 from Forrester, who generously contributied half of the cost, although this is the subject of another story! Forrester died in 1985, after which his wife Gwyneth established the Donald Forrester Trust 1986, a second charity, the Gwyneth Forrester Trust, being established in 2000 following her death in 1999.

Harold H. Ward's name appears on a board, outside and above the double doors at the end of the Dining Room, and a framed photograph of Ward clothed in his Provisional Grand Treasrer regalia hangs in the Boardroom which is also named in his honour. Donald Forrester's portrait hangs inside and above the double doors at the end of the Dining Room, and is the only portrait of a Deputy Provincial Grand Master on display in a Masoinic centre.

On the 24th June, 1717, four London based Lodges met at the Goose & Gridiron Ale House in St Paul's Yard and formed the world's first ever Grand Lodge, electing Anthony Sayer as the first Grand Master and so began the formal regularisation of Freemasonry. As part of the Tercentenary Celebrations, the Masonic Hall has organised an event entitled 'BEHIND CLOSED DOORS' on Sunday 25th June, 2017, where members of the general public and Freemasons are invited to view the building.

The Masonic Hall (Radlett) Limited continues to manage the centre to this day through a Board of volunteer Governors. Over the years, the building has been extended and improved and it now serves over 50 different Lodges and other Masonic units. In addition to the main Temple which is used for regular meetings and ceremonies, there are four rehearsal rooms, a boardroom, lounge bar, and a modern kitchen provides restaurant quality meals with a dining room which comfortably seats up to 70 diners.
